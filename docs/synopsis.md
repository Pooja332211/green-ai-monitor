# Synopsis: Green AI Monitor

## Project Overview

Green AI Monitor is a comprehensive system designed to track and monitor carbon emissions generated by Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) workloads. As AI technologies become increasingly prevalent, their environmental impact, particularly in terms of energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions, has raised significant concerns. This project addresses the need for transparency and accountability in AI's carbon footprint by providing real-time monitoring, data visualization, and educational tools to promote sustainable AI practices.

## Objectives

The primary objectives of the Green AI Monitor project are:

1. **Real-time Emission Tracking**: Utilize advanced carbon tracking libraries to measure CO2 emissions from AI/ML workloads in real-time.
2. **System Resource Monitoring**: Collect and analyze system metrics such as CPU usage, memory consumption, and power draw to correlate with emission data.
3. **Data Visualization**: Provide an intuitive dashboard for users to visualize emission trends, system performance, and optimization opportunities.
4. **Educational Outreach**: Integrate a chatbot component to educate users about carbon emissions, climate change impacts, and reduction strategies specific to AI workloads.
5. **Optimization Suggestions**: Offer actionable recommendations to reduce the environmental impact of AI operations.

## Key Features

- **Backend API**: Built with FastAPI, providing RESTful endpoints for tracking, statistics, logging, and suggestions.
- **Frontend Dashboard**: A React-based web interface displaying live stats, emission charts, and optimization tips.
- **Carbon Tracking Service**: Leverages the CodeCarbon library for accurate emission calculations based on energy consumption.
- **System Monitoring**: Tracks hardware metrics to provide context for emission data.
- **Chatbot Module**: An NLP-powered chatbot for educational interactions about environmental topics.
- **Database Integration**: SQLite-based storage for persistent data on emissions, metrics, and logs.
- **Sample ML Workloads**: Includes examples of common AI tasks (BERT fine-tuning, LSTM training, ResNet training, YOLO inference) for testing and demonstration.

## Technology Stack

- **Backend**: Python, FastAPI, SQLAlchemy, CodeCarbon
- **Frontend**: JavaScript, React
- **Database**: SQLite
- **Chatbot**: Python, NLTK
- **Deployment**: Designed for local development with potential for cloud deployment

## Impact and Significance

Green AI Monitor contributes to the growing field of "Green AI" by providing developers and organizations with tools to quantify and minimize the environmental impact of their AI systems. By making emission data transparent and actionable, the project encourages the adoption of more sustainable AI practices, potentially leading to significant reductions in the carbon footprint of AI technologies worldwide.

## Future Enhancements

Potential areas for expansion include:
- Integration with cloud provider APIs for broader emission tracking
- Machine learning models for predictive emission analysis
- Multi-user support with role-based access control
- Integration with existing CI/CD pipelines for automated monitoring

This project serves as both a practical tool and a foundation for further research into sustainable AI development.
